Sinigang na Baka
Description
Sinigang na Baka (Beef Sinigang) is a classic Filipino dish known for its savory, tangy broth, and tender beef. It's perfect for family gatherings and is known for its balance of flavors, thanks to the use of tamarind as the souring agent and the rich beef flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. beef shank or beef short ribs (cut into pieces)
  • 1 piece Knorr Beef Cube
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (quartered)
  • 3-4 pieces long green chili peppers (siling pang-sigang)
  • 1-2 tablespoons fish sauce (optional, adjust to taste)
  • 2 cups water (adjust to your desired broth consistency)
  • 1 pack (about 40g) sinigang mix (optional, or use fresh tamarind if preferred)
  • 1-2 pieces taro (gabi), peeled and cut into chunks (optional for added texture)
  • 1 cup string beans (sitsaro), trimmed
  • 1 small eggplant, sliced into rounds
  • 1-2 cups kangkong (water spinach) or other leafy greens like bok choy or mustard greens
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Allergens

Fish Allergy
Fish sauce is often used to enhance the flavor of the broth. Individuals with a fish allergy should avoid this ingredient, as it can cause allergic reactions like swelling, hives, or difficulty breathing.
Beef Allergy
Since beef is the main protein in Sinigang na Baka, individuals with a beef allergy should avoid consuming this dish. Symptoms of a beef allergy can range from mild rashes or hives to more severe reactions such as anaphylaxis.

Steps

  1. Boil the beef: In a large pot, add the beef shank or short ribs along with enough water to cover the meat. Bring it to a boil over medium-high heat, then lower the heat and let it simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour or until the meat becomes tender. Skim off any scum or impurities that float to the top.
  2. Add the aromatics: Once the beef is tender, add the onion, tomatoes, and garlic to the pot. Let it simmer for another 10 minutes to infuse the flavors into the broth.
  3. Season the broth: Add the Knorr beef cube to the pot and stir until dissolved. If youâ€™re using fish sauce, add it now for a savory depth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Add the souring agent: Stir in the sinigang mix or fresh tamarind paste if you have it. Adjust the sourness by adding more if desired. If you prefer a milder taste, reduce the amount of sinigang mix.
  5. Cook the vegetables: Add the taro chunks, string beans, and eggplant into the pot. Let it simmer for about 10 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Add the leafy greens and chili: Add the kangkong (or your choice of leafy greens) and the long green chili peppers for a mild kick. Simmer for another 3-5 minutes, until the greens are just tender but still vibrant.
  7. Taste and adjust: Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning. Add more fish sauce, salt, or tamarind if needed for your desired sourness and flavor profile.
  8. Serve: Ladle the beef and vegetables into bowls. Serve hot with steamed rice on the side and enjoy this comforting, savory, and tangy dish!
  9. Enjoy!
