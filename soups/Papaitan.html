Papaitan
Description
Papaitan is a flavorful Filipino dish known for its bold, bitter taste, typically made from goat meat or beef, and seasoned with bile for that distinct bitterness. It’s often enjoyed as a hearty and comforting meal in family gatherings or special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. beef (preferably with innards like tripe or heart, cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 piece Knorr Beef Cube
  • 1 tablespoon bile (from goat or beef; adjust for desired bitterness)
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 piece onion (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 2 pieces thumb-sized ginger (minced)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 pieces long green chili (siling pang-sigang)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 2-3 tablespoons of vinegar (for additional tang)
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (optional)

Allergens

Fish Allergy
Papaitan may also contain fish sauce, which is made from fermented fish. People with fish allergies should avoid this ingredient, as it could trigger an allergic reaction.
Garlic and Onion Allergy
Garlic and onion are essential aromatics in many Filipino dishes, including Papaitan. Some individuals are allergic to these ingredients and may experience reactions like swelling, digestive discomfort, or skin irritation.

Steps

  1. Boil the beef: In a large pot, pour the water and add the beef. Let it boil over medium-high heat. Once the water starts to boil, reduce the heat and allow the beef to simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours until it becomes tender.
  2. Sauté the aromatics: In a separate pan, heat the cooking oil. Sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger until softened and aromatic.
  3. Add the beef mixture: Once the beef is tender, add the sautéed aromatics to the pot with the beef and stir.
  4. Add seasonings: Pour in the fish sauce, soy sauce, and optional vinegar (for added tang). Stir well and let it simmer for another 5-10 minutes for the flavors to meld.
  5. Incorporate the bile: Add the bile to the pot. Adjust the amount according to your preferred bitterness level. Stir to combine and simmer for another 10 minutes.
  6. Add the chili peppers: Add the long green chilies for some spiciness. You can break them up a little to release more flavor if you like it spicier.
  7. Final seasoning: Taste the dish and season with salt and pepper to your liking. If you want a bit more bitterness, feel free to add more bile.
  8. Serve and garnish: Once all the flavors have melded together, transfer the Papaitan to a serving bowl and garnish with fresh herbs if desired.
  9. Enjoy!
