Nilagang Baka
Description
Nilagang Baka is a comforting Filipino beef soup, where beef shank or short ribs are simmered with vegetables such as corn, potatoes, green beans, and pechay. The broth is flavorful yet light, with garlic, onions, and fish sauce adding to its depth. Served with rice, it’s a beloved dish often shared at family gatherings or casual meals.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. beef shank or short ribs (cut into pieces)
  • 2 pieces corn on the cob (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 4 pieces potatoes (peeled and quartered)
  • 1 medium onion (peeled and quartered)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon pepper (or to taste)
  • 1 1/2 cups green beans (trimmed)
  • 2 cups pechay (bok choy or any leafy greens)
  • 1-2 pieces saba bananas (optional, peeled and cut into 2-inch pieces)

Allergens

Fish Sauce Allergy (Patis)
Fish sauce is often used to season Nilagang Baka. People with a fish allergy should avoid this ingredient, as it could trigger an allergic reaction.
Garlic Allergy
Garlic is commonly used for flavoring in Nilagang Baka. Individuals with a garlic allergy may experience symptoms like skin rashes, digestive upset, or swelling.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Beef: In a large pot, add the beef shank or short ribs and cover with water. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for about 1 ½ to 2 hours or until the beef is tender.
  2. Add the Corn: Add the corn pieces and continue to simmer for another 10 minutes to soften.
  3. Season the Broth: Add the onions, garlic, fish sauce, salt, and pepper. Let the soup cook for another 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  4. Add the Vegetables: Add the potatoes and cook for another 10-15 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender. If using saba bananas, add them at this stage and cook until soft.
  5. Finish the Soup: Add the green beans and pechay. Let it cook for another 5 minutes, until the green beans are tender but still vibrant.
  6. Serve: Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Serve hot with steamed rice on the side.
  7. Enjoy!
