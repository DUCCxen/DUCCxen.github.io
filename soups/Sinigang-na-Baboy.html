Sinigang na Baboy
Description
Sinigang na Baboy is a classic Filipino sour soup made with tender pork, vegetables, and a flavorful souring agent, typically tamarind. This dish is perfect for family gatherings, offering a comforting and tangy flavor that everyone loves.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. pork (preferably ribs, belly, or shoulder, cut into chunks)
  • 1 onion (peeled and quartered)
  • 2 tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1-2 long green chili peppers (siling pang-sigang)
  • 1 pack (about 1 oz.) of sinigang mix (optional, or use fresh tamarind)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) leaves
  • 1 cup sitaw (string beans), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1-2 medium eggplants (sliced)
  • 1-2 radishes (labanos), sliced
  • 1-2 small taro roots (gabi), peeled and cubed (optional for added texture)
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Allergens

Fish Allergy
Fish sauce is often used to enhance the flavor of the broth. Individuals with a fish allergy should avoid this ingredient, as it can cause allergic reactions like swelling, hives, or difficulty breathing.
Pork Allergy
The main ingredient in Sinigang na Baboy is pork. Those with a pork allergy may experience allergic reactions such as hives, digestive issues, or more severe symptoms.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Pork: In a large pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the pork and sear it on all sides until the meat starts to brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add the Aromatics: Add the onion and tomatoes to the pot. Cook for about 3-5 minutes, allowing the onions and tomatoes to soften and release their flavors.
  3. Simmer the Pork: Pour in the water and bring to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for 30 minutes or until the pork becomes tender. Skim off any scum or impurities that rise to the surface.
  4. Season the Broth: Add the fish sauce, salt, and pepper to the pot, and stir. Taste the broth and adjust seasoning if necessary. For a tangy flavor, you can add the sinigang mix or tamarind. If using fresh tamarind, you can add a few tamarind pods and cook for an additional 15-20 minutes until the sourness is extracted.
  5. Add the Vegetables: Add the sitaw (string beans), eggplant, radish, and taro root (if using). Let it cook for another 5-10 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Add the Kangkong: Finally, add the kangkong (water spinach) and the long green chili peppers. Let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes until the kangkong is wilted but still bright green.
  7. Serve: Taste the soup one last time and adjust seasoning if needed. Remove from heat and serve hot, perfect with steamed rice.
  8. Enjoy!
