Sinigang na Hipon
Description
Sinigang na Hipon is a popular Filipino dish known for its tangy, savory broth made with shrimp and a variety of fresh vegetables. It combines the sharpness of tamarind with a rich, flavorful base, making it perfect for family gatherings or a comforting meal on a rainy day.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 piece Knorr Shrimp or Pork Cube (for flavor)
  • 10 cups water
  • 1-2 tbsp fish sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2-3 long green chili peppers (siling pang-sigang)
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach), washed and drained
  • 1 cup string beans (cut into 2-inch lengths)
  • 1 large radish (labanos), peeled and sliced
  • 1 medium eggplant (sliced)
  • 1-2 cups tamarind paste or fresh tamarind pods (adjust for sourness)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Allergens

Shellfish Allergy
Fish sauce is often used to enhance the flavor of the broth. Individuals with a fish allergy should avoid this ingredient, as it can cause allergic reactions like swelling, hives, or difficulty breathing.
Fish Sauce Allergy
Fish sauce is often used to season the broth in Sinigang na Hipon. Those with a fish allergy may react to the fish sauce, which can lead to symptoms such as swelling, itching, or more severe reactions.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a large pot, pour in 10 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Add the Knorr shrimp or pork cube, onion, tomatoes, and minced garlic. Let it simmer for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  2. Add Tamarind: If you're using fresh tamarind pods, peel and remove the seeds, then boil them in a separate small pot of water for about 5 minutes, mashing them to extract the pulp. If you're using tamarind paste, simply stir it into the boiling broth. Adjust the amount to suit your preferred level of sourness.
  3. Flavor the Soup: Add fish sauce for a savory, salty depth and season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir the broth and let it simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Add Vegetables: Add the sliced radish, eggplant, and string beans to the pot. Let the vegetables cook for about 7-10 minutes until they are tender but not overcooked.
  5. Cook the Shrimp: Add the shrimp to the pot and cook for 3-5 minutes or until the shrimp turns pink and opaque. Be careful not to overcook the shrimp.
  6. Final Touch: Add the long green chili peppers (siling pang-sigang) and the kangkong (water spinach). Let it cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Taste and Adjust: Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you want it more sour, you can add more tamarind or a little more fish sauce if you prefer a saltier flavor.
  8. Serve: Once everything is cooked, remove from heat and serve hot. Ladle the soup into bowls and enjoy it with steamed rice on the side.
    9. Enjoy!
