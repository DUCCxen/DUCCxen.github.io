Malunggay and Mais Soup
Description
Malunggay and Mais Soup is a delicious and healthy Filipino dish that combines the richness of corn with the nutritious green leaves of malunggay (moringa). This soup is light, flavorful, and packed with vitamins, making it a great comfort food for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Malunggay (Moringa) leaves, washed and drained
  • 2 cups Corn kernels (fresh or canned)
  • 1 lb. Chicken breast or thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 piece Onion (minced)
  • 1 piece Tomato (chopped)
  • 4 cups Chicken broth or water
  • 2 tablespoons Cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon Garlic (minced)
  • 1 piece Knorr Chicken Cube
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons Fish sauce (optional)
  • 1 cup Cabbage or Bok choy (chopped)
  • 1 medium Carrot (sliced thinly)

Allergens

Corn Allergy
Corn is a key ingredient in this soup. Those with a corn allergy may experience symptoms ranging from skin irritation, swelling, stomach upset, or in severe cases, anaphylactic reactions.
Garlic and Onion Allergy
Garlic and onion are commonly used in the soup to enhance its flavor. People with allergies or sensitivities to these ingredients may experience digestive upset, skin reactions, or swelling.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a large pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Saut√© the onion and garlic until fragrant and softened.
  2. Cook the Chicken: Add the chicken pieces to the pot and cook until they are lightly browned.
  3. Add Tomatoes: Add the chopped tomato and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the tomato softens.
  4. Make the Soup Base: Pour in the chicken broth or water and bring it to a boil. Add the Knorr chicken cube to enhance the flavor, then stir until dissolved. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Let the soup simmer for about 10-15 minutes or until the chicken is tender.
  5. Add Corn and Vegetables: Add the corn kernels, carrots, and cabbage or bok choy to the soup. Let it cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Add Malunggay: Once the vegetables are cooked, gently stir in the malunggay leaves. Cook for another 2 minutes, just enough to wilt the malunggay without overcooking it.
  7. Season and Serve: Adjust the seasoning with fish sauce (if using), salt, and pepper to taste. Once everything is well combined, remove from heat and serve hot.
  8. Enjoy!
