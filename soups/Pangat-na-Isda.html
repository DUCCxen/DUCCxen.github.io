Pangat na Isda
Description
Pangat na Isda is a simple yet flavorful Filipino fish dish, known for its savory and tangy broth. It features fish, often bangus or tilapia, cooked with tomatoes, onions, and various seasonings that bring out its natural flavors. It’s perfect for a light meal paired with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs fish (bangus, tilapia, or any firm fish of your choice, cleaned and cut into pieces)
  • 1 medium onion (sliced)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (quartered)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper (or to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) or any leafy greens of your choice (optional)
  • 1-2 pieces long green chili peppers (optional)
  • 1-2 tablespoons vinegar (optional, for a slightly tangy taste)

Allergens

Fish Allergy
The main ingredient in Pangat na Isda is fish. People with a fish allergy can experience symptoms ranging from mild rashes to more severe reactions like anaphylaxis. Common allergens include tilapia, bangus, and other fish varieties used in the dish.
Garlic and Onion Allergy
Garlic and onions are essential flavoring agents in Pangat na Isda. Individuals with an allergy or sensitivity to garlic and onion may experience symptoms like digestive upset, skin rashes, or even more severe reactions such as swelling or breathing difficulties.

Steps

  1. Prepare the fish: Start by cleaning the fish and cutting it into pieces if it isn't pre-cut. Rinse it thoroughly under cold water.
  2. Sauté the aromatics: Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, followed by the sliced onions. Cook until the onions turn soft and translucent. Then, add the tomatoes and cook for about 3-5 minutes until they soften and start to break down.
  3. Add the fish: Add the fish pieces to the pot and carefully stir them around with the sautéed vegetables. Let it cook for 3-4 minutes to allow the fish to absorb the flavors.
  4. Add the broth and seasonings: Pour in the water and bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Once it’s boiling, add the fish sauce, salt, and pepper. Stir gently to combine, making sure the fish is well-coated with the seasonings.
  5. Simmer: Lower the heat to medium-low and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the fish is tender and fully cooked. The broth should have a savory flavor with a hint of sweetness from the tomatoes.
  6. Add greens (optional): If using kangkong or any leafy greens, add them in the last 5 minutes of cooking. Stir them in and cook until wilted.
  7. Optional tangy flavor: For a slightly tangy kick, add the vinegar and long green chili peppers. Let it simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  8. Serve: Once everything is cooked through and well combined, serve the Pangat na Isda with steamed rice and enjoy the comforting flavors of this dish.
  9. Enjoy!
