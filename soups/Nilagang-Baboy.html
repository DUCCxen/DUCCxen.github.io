Nilagang Baboy
Description
Nilagang Baboy is a comforting Filipino dish, perfect for family gatherings and special occasions. It features tender pork simmered with a variety of vegetables in a light, savory broth. This dish is simple but hearty, providing a satisfying taste with every spoonful.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. Pork (preferably pork ribs or pork belly, cut into chunks)
  • 1 piece Onion (peeled and quartered)
  • 4 cloves Garlic (minced)
  • 1 piece Knorr Pork Cube (optional for added flavor)
  • 10 cups Water
  • 2 medium-sized Potatoes (peeled and cut into chunks)
  • 2 ears Corn (cut into 2-3 inch pieces)
  • 1 ½ cups Pechay (bok choy or Chinese cabbage)
  • 1 ½ cups Green beans (sliced into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1 medium-sized Banana heart (optional, sliced thinly)
  • 2 pieces Tomatoes (quartered)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fish sauce or patis to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Allergens

Fish Sauce Allergy (Patis)
Fish sauce is often used to season Nilagang Baboy. People with a fish allergy should avoid this ingredient, as it could trigger an allergic reaction.
Garlic Allergy
Garlic is commonly used for flavoring in Nilagang Baboy. Individuals with a garlic allergy may experience symptoms like skin rashes, digestive upset, or swelling.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Pork: In a large pot, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the pork pieces and sear them for about 3-5 minutes until they get a light brown color on all sides.
  2. Sauté Onions and Garlic: Add the minced garlic and quartered onion to the pot with the pork. Sauté them for 2-3 minutes until they become fragrant.
  3. Add Water and Seasoning: Pour in 10 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Add the Knorr pork cube for added flavor (if using). Season with salt, pepper, and fish sauce to taste. Reduce the heat and let the pork simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the pork is tender.
  4. Add the Vegetables: Once the pork is tender, add the potatoes, corn, and tomatoes to the pot. Let it cook for about 10-15 minutes until the potatoes and corn are tender.
  5. Add Pechay and Other Vegetables: Add the green beans, pechay, and banana heart (if using) into the soup. Let it cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are tender and all the flavors meld together.
  6. Taste and Adjust Seasoning: Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, or fish sauce if necessary.
  7. Serve: Ladle the Nilagang Baboy into bowls and serve with steamed rice. Enjoy this comforting dish with your family and friends!
  8. Enjoy!
