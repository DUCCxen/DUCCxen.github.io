Goto
Description
Goto is a Filipino rice porridge often enjoyed as a breakfast or comfort food, typically made with beef tripe and flavored with ginger, garlic, and onions. It has a savory and slightly rich taste, perfect for a cozy meal. It's also often served with a hard-boiled egg and sometimes topped with chicharrón (crispy pork skin) for added texture.

Ingredients

    Goto:

    • 1 lb beef tripe (cut into small pieces)
    • 1/2 lb beef shank or bones (with marrow)
    • 1 cup glutinous rice (malagkit)
    • 1 medium onion (minced)
    • 1 tablespoon garlic (minced)
    • 2-inch piece of ginger (sliced)
    • 6 cups water (or beef broth)
    • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
    • 1 teaspoon salt (to taste)
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
    • 1-2 pieces hard-boiled eggs (optional)
    • Chicharrón (crispy pork skin), chopped (optional)
    • Green onions or spring onions, chopped (optional for garnish)

Allergens

Beef
Beef is a common allergen, and individuals with a beef allergy may experience symptoms such as hives, digestive issues, or more severe reactions.
Garlic and Onion Allergy
Garlic and onion are commonly used for flavoring in Goto. Individuals sensitive to these ingredients may experience digestive upset, skin reactions, or swelling.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Beef Tripe and Shank: In a large pot, bring 6 cups of water to a boil. Add the beef shank or bones and beef tripe. Boil for about 5-10 minutes to clean, then discard the water. Refill the pot with clean water and bring it to a boil again. Lower the heat and let it simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours until the beef tripe becomes tender. Remove the beef shank and bones, and set them aside.
  2. Cook the Rice: Add the glutinous rice to the pot and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent it from sticking to the bottom. Keep the heat low so the rice cooks into a thick, porridge-like consistency. Add water as needed.
  3. Sauté the Aromatics: In a separate pan, heat a little oil and sauté the garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant and softened.
  4. Combine and Season: Add the sautéed aromatics to the pot with the rice and beef tripe. Stir in fish sauce, salt, and black pepper. Let it simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
  5. Add the Beef Shank: Cut the beef shank or bones into bite-sized pieces, and add them back into the pot. Let everything cook together for another 10 minutes.
  6. Serve: Ladle the goto into bowls, and top each serving with a hard-boiled egg (if using) and chopped chicharrón. Garnish with fresh green onions.
  7. Enjoy!
