Pancit Bihon
Description
Pancit Bihon is a classic Filipino noodle dish that features thin rice noodles sautéed with a savory mix of vegetables, meat, and sometimes shrimp. It's commonly served during celebrations such as birthdays, anniversaries, or family gatherings, offering a flavorful and satisfying dish that is loved by many Filipinos.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. Bihon (rice noodles)
  • 1/2 lb. pork (sliced thinly)
  • 1/2 lb. shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth (or water)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 medium onion (minced)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 medium carrot (julienned)
  • 1/2 cup green beans (sliced into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1/2 cup cabbage (shredded)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs (for garnish, optional)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onions (for garnish)
  • 2 tablespoons calamansi or lemon juice (for a hint of tang)
  • Salt to taste

Allergens

Shellfish
Shrimp is commonly used in Pancit Bihon. People with a shellfish allergy should avoid this dish as it can trigger severe reactions, such as hives, swelling, difficulty breathing, or anaphylaxis.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Soak the Bihon noodles in warm water for 15-20 minutes or until soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté the Meat and Shrimp: Heat the cooking oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the sliced pork and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes. Add the shrimp and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the shrimp turns pink and fully cooked. Remove both the pork and shrimp from the pan and set aside.
  3. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pan, sauté the minced onion and garlic until soft and fragrant, about 2 minutes. Add the julienned carrots and sliced green beans. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Combine the Noodles and Broth: Add the soaked Bihon noodles to the pan with the vegetables. Pour in the chicken broth, soy sauce, and fish sauce, then stir everything together. Let the noodles simmer for about 5-7 minutes, allowing them to absorb the flavors.
  5. Finish the Dish: Once the noodles are tender and have absorbed the broth, return the cooked pork and shrimp to the pan. Stir to combine. Add the shredded cabbage, ground black pepper, and squeeze in the calamansi or lemon juice for a fresh, tangy flavor. Season with salt to taste. Stir everything until well-mixed.
  6. Garnish and Serve: Transfer the Pancit Bihon to a large platter. Garnish with hard-boiled eggs, chopped green onions, and additional calamansi or lemon slices.
  7. Enjoy!
