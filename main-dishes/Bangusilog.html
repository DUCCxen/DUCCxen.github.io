Bangusilog
Description
Bangusilog is a delicious Filipino breakfast combo of fried milkfish (bangus), garlic fried rice (sinangag), and a fried egg (itlog). It's a simple yet flavorful meal that packs a punch with savory, garlicky, and tangy elements.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole bangus (milkfish), cleaned and butterflied (about 1 1/2 lbs.)
  • 3 cups cooked rice (preferably cold or day-old rice)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar (for marinating the bangus)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon calamansi juice (or lemon if calamansi is unavailable)
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil (for frying the bangus)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil (for garlic fried rice)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Toyomansi (soy sauce with calamansi or lemon) for dipping
  • Optional: Bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) for extra flavor

Allergens

Fish
Bangus is the main ingredient for Bangusilog so those with fish allergies should be cautious.

Steps

  1. Marinate the Bangus: In a bowl, combine the vinegar, soy sauce, calamansi juice, and pepper. Mix well. Coat the bangus with this marinade and let it sit for about 15–20 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the fish.
  2. Prepare the Garlic Fried Rice: Heat 2 tablespoons of cooking oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Sauté the minced garlic until golden brown and aromatic. Add the cold, day-old rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps. Stir thoroughly to combine with the garlic, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Sauté for about 5–7 minutes until heated through and slightly crispy. Set aside.
  3. Cook the Bangus: In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of cooking oil over medium-high heat. Fry the marinated bangus for about 4–5 minutes on each side, or until the skin is crispy and the flesh is cooked through. Be gentle when flipping the fish to avoid breaking it.
  4. Fry the Eggs: Heat a small amount of oil in a pan and fry the eggs to your liking (sunny-side-up or scrambled). Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. Assemble the Bangusilog: On a plate, serve a generous portion of garlic fried rice (sinangag). Place the fried bangus on the side and top with a fried egg.
  6. Optional: Serve with a side of toyomansi (soy sauce and calamansi or lemon) and a small portion of bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) for an authentic Filipino touch.
  7. Enjoy!
