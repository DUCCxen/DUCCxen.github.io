Lechon Kawali
Description
Lechon Kawali is a popular Filipino pork dish known for its crispy, golden-brown skin and tender, juicy meat. It is made by deep-frying pork belly (liempo) that has been boiled until tender, then air-dried to achieve the perfect crunch when fried. The result is a flavorful contrast between the crunchy outer layer and the succulent meat inside. This dish is commonly served with liver sauce (lechon sauce), spiced vinegar, or soy sauce with calamansi and chili for added flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds pork belly, cut into small pieces
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 cups oil for frying, or as needed

Allergens

Gluten
If the pork is seasoned or coated with flour before frying, it may contain gluten, which can trigger reactions in people with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease.

Steps

  1. Combine pork belly, salt, garlic, bay leaves, and pepper in a pot.
  2. Pour enough water into the pot. Let it boil for 30-45 minutes or until the skin is tender.
  3. Drain the water and let the pork sit at room temperature to air-dry.
  4. Heat oil in a pot to deep fry the meat.
  5. Put the pork in the pot. Deep fry for 3-5 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Remove the pork from the pot and drain the excess oil on a paper towel.
  7. Serve on a plate. Enjoy.
