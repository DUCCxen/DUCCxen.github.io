Pancit Habhab
Description
Pancit Habhab is a traditional Filipino noodle dish from the province of Quezon. It is known for being served on banana leaves and enjoyed with simple yet flavorful ingredients like rice noodles, pork, shrimp, and vegetables. The dish is loved for its easy preparation, savory flavor, and unique presentation.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. Pancit habhab noodles (or any stir-fry rice noodles)
  • 1/2 lb. pork belly (sliced thinly)
  • 1/2 lb. shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 1 medium onion (minced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 medium carrot (julienned)
  • 1/2 cup green beans (sliced into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1/2 cup cabbage (shredded)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 piece chicken bouillon cube (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons calamansi juice or lemon juice
  • Banana leaves (for serving)
  • Salt to taste

Allergens

Shellfish
Shrimp is commonly used in Pancit Habhab. People with a shellfish allergy should avoid this dish as it can trigger severe reactions, such as hives, swelling, difficulty breathing, or anaphylaxis.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Boil the Pancit habhab noodles (or any rice noodles) in a pot with water for about 3-5 minutes, or until al dente. Drain the noodles and set aside.
  2. Cook the Meat and Shrimp: Heat the cooking oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the sliced pork belly and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes. Remove and set aside. In the same pan, add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and fully cooked. Remove and set aside.
  3. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pan, sauté the minced onion and garlic until soft and fragrant, about 2 minutes. Add the julienned carrots and green beans. Stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Combine the Noodles and Broth: Add the cooked noodles to the pan with the sautéed vegetables. Pour in the chicken broth, soy sauce, fish sauce, and ground black pepper. Stir well to combine. Let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes, ensuring the noodles absorb the broth and seasonings.
  5. Finish the Dish: Add the cooked pork belly, shrimp, and shredded cabbage to the pan. Stir to combine and let it cook for another 2-3 minutes until everything is well-mixed and the cabbage has softened. Squeeze in calamansi or lemon juice for a fresh, tangy kick. Season with salt to taste.
  6. Serve and Garnish: To serve, line a plate with a banana leaf and place the Pancit Habhab on top. This traditional presentation adds a distinct flavor and feel to the dish. You can also serve this dish with extra calamansi or lemon wedges on the side for added zest.
  7. Enjoy!
