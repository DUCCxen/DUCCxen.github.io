Longsilog
Description
Longsilog is a classic Filipino breakfast dish that combines longganisa (Filipino sweet sausage), garlic fried rice (sinangag), and a fried egg (itlog). The sweet and savory flavors from the longganisa complement the garlic-infused rice, making it a hearty and satisfying meal often enjoyed in the morning. It is a popular dish in Filipino homes and restaurants, often served with vinegar on the side.

Ingredients

  • For the Longganisa (Sweet Sausage):
  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp paprika (optional)
  • 1 tbsp vinegar (optional)
  • For the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):
  • 3 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • For the Itlog (Fried Egg):
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil or butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Allergens

Garlic and Onion
Garlic and onion are commonly used in the preparation of the garlic fried rice (sinangag). Some people may have sensitivities or allergies to these ingredients, leading to digestive distress or skin reactions.

Steps

  1. Make the Longganisa: In a bowl, combine the ground pork, brown sugar, soy sauce, minced garlic, salt, black pepper, paprika, and vinegar (optional). Mix well until everything is fully incorporated. Shape the mixture into small sausage links or patties. Heat a skillet over medium heat and cook the longganisa for 4-5 minutes per side, or until cooked through and slightly caramelized. Set aside.
  2. Make the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice): In the same skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and saut√© until fragrant and golden brown, about 1-2 minutes. Add the cooked rice and stir-fry for 3-5 minutes, breaking up any clumps, until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Set aside.
  3. Make the Itlog (Fried Egg): Heat oil or butter in a non-stick frying pan over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the pan and fry until the whites are set but the yolk is still runny, or cook to your desired doneness. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Assemble the Longsilog: Serve a portion of garlic fried rice on each plate. Place the cooked longganisa on the side. Top with a freshly fried egg.
  5. Enjoy!
