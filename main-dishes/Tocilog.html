Tocilog
Description
Tocilog is a popular Filipino breakfast dish made up of Tocino (sweet cured pork), Sinangag (garlic fried rice), and Itlog (fried egg). This dish is known for its sweet, savory, and hearty flavor, making it a favorite among Filipinos, especially for breakfast or brunch.

Ingredients

    Tocino (Sweet Cured Pork):

    • 1 lb. pork belly or pork shoulder (sliced thinly)
    • 1/4 cup soy sauce
    • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
    • 1 tablespoon vinegar
    • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
    • 1 tablespoon cooking oil (for frying)

    Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):

    • 3 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
    • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
    • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
    • Salt and pepper to taste

    Itlog (Fried Egg):

    • 3 large eggs
    • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Allergens

Garlic
Garlic is used in both the fried rice (sinangag). Individuals with a garlic allergy might experience symptoms like digestive upset, skin irritation, or more severe reactions.

Steps

  1. Marinate the Tocino (Sweet Cured Pork): In a bowl, combine soy sauce, brown sugar, vinegar, minced garlic, and ground black pepper. Stir until the sugar dissolves. Add the sliced pork to the marinade and mix well, ensuring the pork is well-coated. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for better flavor.
  2. Cook the Tocino (Sweet Cured Pork): Heat the cooking oil in a pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated pork and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the pork is golden brown and slightly caramelized. Set aside.
  3. Prepare the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice): In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of cooking oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and saut√© until golden brown and fragrant. Add the cooked rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps, and stir to combine with the garlic. Let the rice fry for 5-7 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Once done, remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Cook the Itlog (Fried Egg): In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of cooking oil over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the pan and cook to your preferred doneness, whether sunny-side-up, over-easy, or scrambled.
  5. Assemble the Tocilog: On a plate, serve a portion of garlic fried rice (sinangag) as the base. Add the cooked tocino (sweet cured pork) on top of the rice. Place the fried egg (itlog) on the side or on top of the rice, as preferred.
  6. Enjoy!
