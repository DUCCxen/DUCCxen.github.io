Sisig
Description
Sisig is a popular Filipino dish made from chopped and seasoned parts of a pig’s head, usually served on a sizzling plate. It’s a savory, slightly spicy dish that’s typically enjoyed with rice or as an appetizer. The dish has its roots in Pampanga but has become a beloved comfort food across the Philippines, often served during celebrations, gatherings, and even casual meals.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. pork belly (or pig's head, including ears and snout)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 piece onion (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon calamansi or lemon juice
  • 1-2 teaspoons minced chili (optional, depending on your spice preference)
  • 2 teaspoons cooking oil
  • 1 piece egg (optional, for topping)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 piece bay leaf (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions (for garnish)

Allergens

Chili Pepper Allergy
Chili peppers are sometimes added to Sisig to give it a spicy kick. Those with a capsaicin (chili) allergy might experience skin irritation, digestive distress, or other symptoms when consuming spicy foods.

Steps

  1. Boil the Pork: In a large pot, add the pork belly (or pig’s head), soy sauce, and bay leaf. Fill the pot with enough water to cover the meat and bring it to a boil. Lower the heat and let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the pork is tender. You can also add some vinegar to help tenderize the meat. Once cooked, remove the meat from the pot and let it cool slightly before chopping it into small pieces.
  2. Prepare the Sisig: Heat the cooking oil in a skillet or frying pan over medium heat. Add the chopped pork, and sauté until it becomes crispy and golden brown. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Add the minced garlic and onions and cook until fragrant and soft.
  3. Season the Sisig: Once the pork is crispy, add the vinegar and let it cook for another 2-3 minutes to allow the vinegar to cook off. Stir in the mayonnaise, calamansi or lemon juice, and minced chili (if using). Season with salt and pepper to taste. Continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes until everything is well combined.
  4. Finish the Sisig: Optionally, you can top the sisig with a raw egg. Crack the egg directly on the hot sisig mixture, then cover the pan to let it cook for about 1-2 minutes until the egg is just slightly set.
  5. Serve and Garnish: Transfer the sisig to a sizzling plate or a serving dish. Garnish with chopped green onions for a fresh flavor.
  6. Enjoy!
