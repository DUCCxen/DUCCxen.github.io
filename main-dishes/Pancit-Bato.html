Pancit Bato
Description
Pancit Bato is a popular Filipino noodle dish from Bicol, known for its unique and delicious flavor. The dish features thick rice noodles called "bato," sautéed with a mix of meats, vegetables, and sometimes seafood. Pancit Bato is often served during special occasions, such as birthdays or celebrations, offering a savory and slightly spicy flavor that Bicolanos are known for.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. Pancit Bato noodles (or other thick rice noodles)
  • 1 lb. pork belly, sliced into strips
  • 1/2 lb. shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 lb. chicken breast, shredded (optional)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 medium onion, minced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cup green beans, sliced into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong) (optional)
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp calamansi or lemon juice (for a hint of tang)
  • Salt to taste
  • Hard-boiled eggs for garnish (optional)
  • Sliced calamansi or lemon for garnish
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Allergens

Shellfish
Shrimp is commonly used in Pancit Bato. People with a shellfish allergy should avoid this dish as it can trigger severe reactions, such as hives, swelling, difficulty breathing, or anaphylaxis.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Cook the Pancit Bato noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté the Meat: Heat the cooking oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the sliced pork belly and cook until browned and crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Add the shrimp and chicken (if using) and cook for another 3-4 minutes until the shrimp turns pink and the chicken is fully cooked. Remove the meat and seafood from the pan and set aside.
  3. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pan, sauté the minced onion until soft and fragrant, about 2 minutes. Add the julienned carrots and sliced green beans. Stir-fry for another 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.
  4. Combine the Noodles and Broth: Add the cooked Pancit Bato noodles to the pan with the vegetables. Pour in the chicken broth and soy sauce, then stir everything together. Add the fish sauce, ground black pepper, and shrimp paste (optional) for added flavor. Let the noodles simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Finish and Season: Once the noodles have absorbed the flavors and most of the liquid has been absorbed, return the cooked meat and shrimp to the pan. Stir to combine. Add the shredded cabbage and squeeze in the calamansi or lemon juice for a fresh, tangy flavor. Season with salt to taste.
  6. Garnish and Serve: Serve the Pancit Bato on a large platter, garnishing with hard-boiled eggs (if using), sliced calamansi or lemon, and chopped green onions.
  7. Enjoy!
