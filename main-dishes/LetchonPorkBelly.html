Letchon Pork Belly
Description
Lechon Pork Belly is a Filipino favorite often enjoyed during celebrations, special occasions, or family gatherings. It features a tender, flavorful, and crispy roast pork belly, marinated with a blend of spices and herbs. The combination of crispy skin and juicy meat is a true crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

  • 2 to 3 lbs. pork belly (boneless, skin-on)
  • 1 tablespoon salt (for seasoning)
  • 1 tablespoon pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 5 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 stalks lemongrass (bruised or chopped)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for roasting)
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil (for basting)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, for a slight sweetness)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional for added umami)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Juice of 1 calamansi or lemon (for added tang)

Allergens

Spices
Certain spices like chili, paprika, or cumin, which might be used in the seasoning of pork belly, can trigger allergies or intolerances in some people, leading to symptoms like skin rashes or digestive distress.

Steps

  1. Marinate the Pork Belly: In a bowl, mix together the salt, pepper, paprika, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, onion, and fish sauce. Rub this marinade generously over the pork belly, making sure it’s evenly coated. Stuff the lemongrass and bay leaves inside the pork belly. Let the pork belly marinate for at least 2 hours (overnight for best flavor).
  2. Prepare for Roasting: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). After marinating, roll the pork belly tightly and tie it with kitchen twine to secure it. This helps maintain its shape and ensures even cooking. Brush the outside of the rolled pork belly with a little olive oil to help crisp the skin during roasting.
  3. Roast the Pork Belly: Place the pork belly on a rack or roasting pan. Add about 1/2 cup of water at the bottom of the pan to keep the pork moist during the roasting process. Roast the pork belly in the preheated oven for about 1 1/2 to 2 hours, or until the meat is cooked through and the skin begins to crisp up. For extra crispy skin, increase the temperature to 400°F (200°C) during the last 20–30 minutes of roasting. Keep an eye on it to avoid burning.
  4. Baste and Caramelize: About halfway through roasting, baste the pork belly with cooking oil to help achieve a golden, crispy skin. If you want a slight sweetness to balance out the savory flavors, brush the pork belly with a mix of sugar and vinegar in the last 10 minutes of roasting.
  5. Rest and Serve: Once done, let the pork belly rest for 10 minutes before slicing. This will help the juices settle and make slicing easier. Slice the pork belly into bite-sized pieces and serve with your choice of dipping sauces (soy sauce with calamansi or vinegar with garlic and chili) and a side of pancit or steamed rice.
  6. Enjoy!
