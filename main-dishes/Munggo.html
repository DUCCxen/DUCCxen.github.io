Munggo
Description
Munggo (also known as Mung Bean Soup) is a traditional Filipino dish made from mung beans, typically served as a hearty soup or stew. It's a nutritious and flavorful dish often enjoyed with rice and sometimes accompanied by fish, pork, or shrimp. Munggo has roots in pre-colonial Filipino cuisine and continues to be a comfort food enjoyed in households throughout the country.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup mung beans (munggo)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 lb pork (or shrimp), chopped (optional)
  • 1 cup spinach or malunggay leaves
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce or soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 tsp ground pepper
  • 1/2 tsp paprika (optional for flavor)
  • 1 cup pork broth or water (for extra flavor)

Allergens

Spices (Paprika, Pepper)
Spices like paprika or black pepper, which may be used to season munggo, can sometimes trigger allergic reactions, particularly in individuals sensitive to certain spices. Symptoms can include skin irritation or digestive discomfort.

Steps

  1. Cook the Mung Beans: Rinse the mung beans thoroughly under cold water. In a pot, combine the mung beans and 4 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 30-45 minutes, or until the beans are tender. You can also use a pressure cooker to speed up the cooking process (about 10 minutes on high pressure).
  2. Prepare the Aromatics and Pork: In a separate pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and tomato, sautéing until the vegetables soften and become fragrant, about 3-4 minutes. If using pork, add the chopped pork and cook until the pork is browned and cooked through. Season with fish sauce or soy sauce, salt, and pepper.
  3. Combine Mung Beans and Pork: Once the mung beans are cooked and tender, add the sautéed onion, garlic, tomato, and pork mixture into the pot of mung beans. Stir everything together and let it simmer for another 10-15 minutes so the flavors can meld.
  4. Add Greens and Final Seasoning: Add the spinach or malunggay leaves to the pot. Simmer for another 2-3 minutes, just until the leaves are wilted. Adjust the seasoning with salt, pepper, and a pinch of paprika for extra flavor, if desired.
  5. Serve: Serve the munggo hot, typically with a side of steamed rice. You can also serve it with fried fish or crispy pork belly for added flavor.
  6. Enjoy!
