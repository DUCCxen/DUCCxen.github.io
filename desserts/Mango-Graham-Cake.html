Mango Graham Cake
Description
Mango Graham Cake is a delicious and no-bake Filipino dessert that features layers of graham crackers, sweetened cream, and fresh mango slices. Itâ€™s a refreshing treat often served during special occasions or gatherings. The combination of creamy filling and fresh fruit makes it a crowd favorite!

Ingredients

  • 1 pack Graham crackers (about 10-12 pieces per layer)
  • 2 large ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced
  • 2 cups all-purpose cream
  • 1 can (14 oz) condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup whipping cream (optional for extra creaminess)
  • 1/4 cup butter (melted)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract (optional)
  • 1/4 cup crushed graham crackers (for topping)

Allergens

Coconut
Kutsinta is often topped with grated coconut or latik (coconut curds). Coconut can cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals, including skin irritation or digestive issues.

Steps

  1. Prepare the base layer: In a shallow rectangular or square dish, arrange a layer of Graham crackers at the bottom, making sure they cover the entire surface.
  2. Make the cream filling: In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose cream, condensed milk, and whipping cream (if using). Whisk until smooth and well-combined. You can add a little vanilla extract for extra flavor if desired.
  3. Layer the cake: Pour a portion of the cream mixture over the Graham crackers layer, spreading it evenly. Then add a layer of sliced mangoes on top of the cream. Repeat the process: add another layer of Graham crackers, followed by more cream, and then another layer of mango slices.
  4. Chill the cake: Once all layers are built, spread the remaining cream mixture on top and garnish with a layer of crushed Graham crackers. Refrigerate the cake for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld and the cake to set.
  5. Serve: Slice and serve chilled for a refreshing and creamy dessert!
