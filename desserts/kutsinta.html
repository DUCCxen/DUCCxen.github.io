Kutsinta
Description
Kutsinta is a popular Filipino steamed rice cake known for its chewy texture and rich brown color. Made from rice flour, brown sugar, and lye water, it has a distinct chewy and slightly gelatinous texture. Traditionally topped with grated coconut or latik, it is often served as a snack or dessert, especially during merienda or special occasions. The name "Kutsinta" is derived from the Hokkien word "kueh tsin tao," reflecting its Chinese influence.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tsp lye water (lihia)
  • Grated coconut or latik (for topping)

Allergens

Coconut
Kutsinta is often topped with grated coconut or latik (coconut curds). Coconut can cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals, including skin irritation or digestive issues.

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine rice flour, all-purpose flour, and brown sugar. Mix well.
  2. Gradually add water while stirring to avoid lumps.
  3. Add lye water and mix until the batter is smooth.
  4. Strain the batter to ensure it is free of lumps.
  5. Pour the batter into small molds or muffin tins, filling them about 3/4 full.
  6. Steam the kutsinta for 20-25 minutes or until the tops are firm and shiny.
  7. Remove from the steamer and let them cool slightly.
  8. Top with grated coconut or latik before serving.
