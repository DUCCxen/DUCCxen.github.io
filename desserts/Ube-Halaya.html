Ube Halaya
Description
Ube Halaya is a thick, sweet jam made from purple yam, a staple ingredient in Filipino desserts. It is a popular treat during special occasions and is often used as a filling or topping for other desserts like Halo-Halo and Bibingka. The name “Ube Halaya” comes from the Filipino word for purple yam (“ube”) and the Spanish word for jam (“halaya”). Ube Halaya is similar to other root-based desserts like sweet potato jam but is distinct for its vibrant purple color and rich, creamy texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups grated purple yam (ube)
  • 1 can (390g) condensed milk
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup sugar

Allergens

Dairy
Ube halaya often contains butter or sometimes condensed milk. People with dairy allergies or lactose intolerance should be cautious.

Steps

  1. Cook grated ube in a pan with coconut milk until soft.
  2. Add condensed milk, sugar, and butter. Stir continuously until thick.
  3. Transfer to a container and let it cool. Serve as a spread or dessert.
