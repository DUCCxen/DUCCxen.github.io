Bukayo
Description
Bukayo is a sweet Filipino treat made from shredded coconut cooked with sugar until it forms a thick, sticky, and chewy mixture. It is often enjoyed as a snack or dessert and can be served as-is or paired with other Filipino delicacies like suman. Bukayo has a rich, coconut flavor and is sometimes infused with pandan or vanilla for added depth. It is a beloved treat, especially for those who enjoy the natural sweetness of coconut in its simplest form.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shredded fresh coconut (or 1 pack of desiccated coconut)
  • 1 cup brown sugar (or white sugar, if preferred)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional, for extra flavor)
  • A pinch of salt (optional, for balance)
  • Pandan leaves (optional, for fragrance)

Allergens

Coconut
Coconut is the main ingredient in Bukayo. Some people may have an allergy to coconut, which can cause reactions like skin irritation, digestive issues, or even more severe symptoms.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Coconut: If using fresh coconut, shred it finely. If using desiccated coconut, measure out the required amount.
  2. Cook the Bukayo:
    1. In a pan over medium heat, combine the shredded coconut, sugar, and water. Stir well to mix.
    2. If you have pandan leaves, tie a few leaves in a knot and add them to the mixture to infuse a subtle fragrance. If not using pandan, skip this step.
  3. Simmer the Mixture: Cook the coconut and sugar mixture over low to medium heat, stirring frequently to prevent burning. Continue to cook until the sugar has dissolved completely and the mixture becomes thick and sticky. This usually takes about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Add Flavoring: Once the mixture has thickened, add a pinch of salt and vanilla extract, if desired, for extra flavor. Stir well to combine.
  5. Cool and Serve: Once the mixture is thick and sticky, remove it from the heat. Allow it to cool slightly before transferring it to a serving dish. You can enjoy it warm or at room temperature.
  6. Optional Serving Suggestions: Bukayo can be eaten as is or served with a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds for extra crunch. It can also be used as a topping for other Filipino desserts like suman (sticky rice) or even ice cream.
