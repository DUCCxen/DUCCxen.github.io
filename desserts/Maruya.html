Maruya
Description
Maruya is a Filipino snack made of ripe bananas (typically saba bananas), coated in a sweet batter, and deep-fried until golden and crispy. It’s often enjoyed as a snack or dessert.

Ingredients

  • 4 ripe saba bananas (or plantains)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • 2/3 cup water (or enough to form a batter)
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk (optional for extra flavor)
  • Cooking oil (for frying)

Allergens

Dairy
Maruya contains Coconut milk if added. People with dairy allergies or lactose intolerance should be cautious.

Steps

  1. Prepare the bananas: Peel and slice the saba bananas into thin rounds or diagonally, depending on your preference.
  2. Make the batter: In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the egg, vanilla extract (optional), and coconut milk (optional). Gradually mix in water until you achieve a smooth batter that is thick enough to coat the bananas.
  3. Coat the bananas: Dip each sliced banana into the batter, making sure it’s fully coated.
  4. Heat the oil: In a deep frying pan, heat enough cooking oil to submerge the bananas over medium heat. The oil should be hot enough that when a small drop of batter is added, it sizzles immediately.
  5. Fry the bananas: Carefully drop the coated banana slices into the hot oil, frying in batches to avoid overcrowding. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  6. Drain the excess oil: Once the Maruya is golden and crispy, remove from the oil and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  7. Serve warm: Serve the Maruya immediately, optionally dusted with powdered sugar or served with a drizzle of honey for added sweetness.
