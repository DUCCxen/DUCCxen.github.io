Leche Flan
Description
Leche Flan is a rich and creamy caramel custard dessert that is a staple in Filipino celebrations, especially during fiestas and holidays. The name “Leche Flan” comes from the Spanish words for “milk” and “flan,” reflecting its Spanish influence. It is made from egg yolks, condensed milk, and fresh milk, with a layer of caramelized sugar at the bottom. Leche Flan is often served as a dessert on its own or as a topping for other Filipino treats like Halo-Halo. It is similar to the Spanish flan but is richer and denser due to the use of more egg yolks and condensed milk.

Ingredients

  • 10 egg yolks
  • 1 can (390g) condensed milk
  • 1 cup fresh milk or evaporated milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup sugar (for caramel)

Allergens

Dairy
Since leche flan contains condensed milk, evaporated milk, or regular milk, which can cause issues for those with dairy allergies.

Steps

  1. Melt sugar in a llanera (or mold) over low heat until it turns into golden caramel. Swirl to coat the bottom evenly. Let it cool.
  2. In a bowl, mix egg yolks, condensed milk, fresh milk, and vanilla extract. Strain the mixture to remove lumps.
  3. Pour the mixture into the caramel-coated mold.
  4. Steam for 30-40 minutes or until set. Let it cool, then refrigerate before serving.
