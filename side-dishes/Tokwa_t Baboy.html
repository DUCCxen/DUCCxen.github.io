Tokwa't Baboy
Description
Tokwa't Baboy is a popular Filipino appetizer made of fried tofu (tokwa) and boiled pork, usually served with a tangy soy-vinegar dipping sauce. It is commonly enjoyed as a side dish or pulutan (beer match).

Ingredients

  • 250g firm tofu, cubed
  • 250g pork belly or pork ears, boiled and sliced
  • ½ cup vinegar
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small chili (optional), chopped
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil for frying

Allergens

Soy
Contains tofu and soy sauce, which are both made from soybeans.
Gluten
Soy sauce may contain gluten, depending on the brand.
Sulfites
Vinegar may contain sulfites, which can trigger allergies in some individuals.
Pork
Contains pork, which may not be suitable for individuals with dietary restrictions.

Steps

  1. Boil the pork belly or pork ears until tender. Slice into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the tofu cubes until golden brown. Drain excess oil.
  3. In a bowl, mix vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, onion, chili (if using), sugar, salt, and pepper to make the dipping sauce.
  4. Combine the fried tofu and sliced pork in a serving dish.
  5. Drizzle with the sauce or serve it on the side.
  6. Enjoy as a side dish or appetizer!
