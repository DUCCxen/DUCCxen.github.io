Ginisang Ampalaya
Description
Ginisang Ampalaya is a Filipino stir-fried dish made with bitter melon, eggs, and sometimes tomatoes and ground meat. It is a healthy dish known for its slightly bitter taste but is packed with nutrients.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium bitter melons (ampalaya), sliced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp salt (for soaking ampalaya)
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Allergens

Eggs
Ginisang Ampalaya contains eggs, which are a common allergen.
Soy
Soy sauce is used in this dish and may cause allergic reactions for those sensitive to soy.
Tomatoes
Tomatoes are part of the nightshade family and can trigger allergies in some individuals.

Steps

  1. Slice the ampalaya thinly and soak in water with salt for 10-15 minutes to reduce bitterness. Rinse and drain.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Sauté garlic, onion, and tomato until soft.
  3. Add the ampalaya and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Pour in the soy sauce and season with black pepper.
  5. Push the ampalaya to the side of the pan and pour the beaten eggs. Let them set slightly before stirring to combine.
  6. Continue cooking for another 2 minutes or until eggs are fully cooked.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.
