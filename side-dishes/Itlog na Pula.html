Itlog na Pula
Description
Itlog na Pula, or salted egg, is a Filipino delicacy made by curing duck eggs in a brine solution or a salt-based mixture for several weeks. It is commonly eaten with tomatoes, used in salads, or as a topping for various dishes.

Ingredients

  • 6 duck eggs
  • 1 cup salt
  • 4 cups water
  • Optional: Red food coloring

Allergens

Eggs
Duck eggs are the main ingredient and may cause allergic reactions in some individuals.
Sodium Sensitivity
Salted eggs are high in sodium, which may be a concern for individuals with hypertension or sodium-related health issues.
Food Coloring (if used)
Some commercial salted eggs use red food coloring, which may cause reactions in individuals sensitive to artificial dyes.

Steps

  1. In a pot, heat water and dissolve salt completely to create a brine solution. Let it cool.
  2. Place the duck eggs in a clean glass jar or container.
  3. Pour the cooled brine over the eggs, ensuring they are fully submerged. Use a weight if necessary.
  4. Seal the container and store in a cool, dark place for 3-4 weeks.
  5. After curing, boil the eggs for 10-12 minutes.
  6. Optionally, soak the shells in red food coloring for a traditional look.
  7. Peel and serve as is, or pair with tomatoes and rice.
