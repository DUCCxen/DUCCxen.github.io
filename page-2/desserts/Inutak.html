Inutak
Description
Inutak is a decadent Filipino delicacy from the Taguig area in Metro Manila. This rich, chewy rice cake is made from glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar, then topped with thick coconut cream (kakang gata) before being baked or broiled until a toasty, slightly burnt layer forms on top. The name inutak comes from the Tagalog word utak (brain) because the browned coconut cream topping resembles brain matter. Inutak is often enjoyed warm and paired with ice cream for an extra indulgent treat.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup thick coconut cream (kakang gata)

Allergens

Dairy
Inutak contains dairy which can cause issues for those with dairy allergies.

Steps

  1. Prepare the mixture: In a pot over medium heat, combine glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, condensed milk, sugar, salt, and vanilla extract. Stir continuously until thick and smooth.
  2. Transfer to a baking dish: Pour the mixture into a greased baking dish or pan, spreading it evenly.
  3. Add the coconut cream: Pour the thick coconut cream on top, spreading it gently to cover the surface.
  4. Bake: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake for 20–25 minutes until the top is slightly firm.
  5. Broil: Switch to the broil setting and continue baking for another 5–10 minutes until the coconut cream forms a golden-brown, slightly burnt layer.
  6. Cool and serve: Let it cool slightly before slicing. Serve warm or with ice cream for extra richness.
