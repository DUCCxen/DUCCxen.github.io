Taho
Description
Taho is a beloved Filipino street food made with warm, silky tofu, sweet arnibal (caramelized brown sugar syrup), and chewy sago pearls. It is traditionally sold by vendors carrying large containers and calling out "Taho!" in the early mornings. This comforting and lightly sweet treat is often enjoyed as a breakfast or snack.

Ingredients

  • For the Silken Tofu:
  • 1 block silken tofu (store-bought or homemade)
  • For the Arnibal (Sweet Syrup):
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • For the Sago Pearls:
  • 1/2 cup small sago pearls
  • 4 cups water (for boiling)

Allergens

Steps

  1. Prepare the Sago Pearls: Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a medium pot. Add the sago pearls and cook for 20–30 minutes until they turn translucent. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Once cooked, drain and rinse the sago pearls under cold water. Set aside.
  2. Make the Arnibal (Sweet Syrup): In a saucepan over low heat, combine brown sugar and water. Stir until the sugar is fully dissolved, and let it simmer for 5–7 minutes until it thickens slightly. Add the vanilla extract and mix well. Remove from heat and set aside to cool slightly.
  3. Steam or Heat the Silken Tofu: Steam the silken tofu for about 5 minutes until warm. Alternatively, you can microwave it for 1–2 minutes until heated through.
  4. Assemble the Taho: In a serving glass or cup, add a generous spoonful of sago pearls at the bottom. Add the warm silken tofu on top of the sago pearls. Drizzle the arnibal (sweet syrup) generously over the tofu and sago pearls.
  5. Serve and Enjoy: Stir gently to mix the syrup, tofu, and sago pearls together, and enjoy your warm, comforting Taho.
