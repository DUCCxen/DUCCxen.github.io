Camote Cue
Description
Camote Cue is a popular Filipino street food that features fried sweet potatoes (camote) coated in caramelized brown sugar, giving it a crispy, sweet exterior. It’s a simple yet delicious snack enjoyed by many. Here’s a recipe to recreate this Filipino treat!

Ingredients

  • 4-5 medium-sized camote (sweet potatoes)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1 tbsp butter (optional, for extra flavor)
  • A pinch of salt (optional, to balance the sweetness)

Allergens

Steps

  1. Prepare the camote: Peel the sweet potatoes and slice them into thick rounds or sticks (about 1/2 inch thick). Rinse them in cold water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  2. Coat in sugar: In a shallow dish, place the brown sugar. Roll each camote slice in the sugar until fully coated. Make sure each piece is generously covered with the sugar for a crispy, caramelized crust.
  3. Heat oil: In a large frying pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. You can also add a tablespoon of butter if you'd like a richer flavor.
  4. Fry the camote: Once the oil is hot, carefully add the sugar-coated camote slices to the pan. Fry them for about 4-6 minutes on each side, until golden brown and crispy. The sugar will begin to caramelize, giving the camote a sweet, crunchy coating.
  5. Serve and enjoy: Once fried, remove the camote from the pan and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil. Allow them to cool slightly before serving.
