Banana Cue
Description
Banana Cue is a beloved Filipino street food snack, featuring fried caramelized bananas coated in brown sugar. It's simple to make and delivers a satisfying sweet crunch. Traditionally, saba bananas (a variety of plantain) are used in this dish, which gives it a unique flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4-6 ripe saba bananas (or plantains if unavailable)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1 tbsp butter (optional, for extra flavor)
  • A pinch of salt (optional, to balance the sweetness)

Allergens

Dairy
This cuisuine contains Dairy(Butter) though you can avoid this by not applying Butter

Steps

  1. Peel the bananas and slice them in half lengthwise, or leave them whole, depending on your preference.
  2. In a shallow dish, pour the brown sugar. Roll each banana slice in the sugar until fully coated.
  3. In a large frying pan or wok, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Optionally, add a tablespoon of butter for a richer taste.
  4. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the sugar-coated bananas to the pan. Fry them for about 3-5 minutes on each side, until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Once fried, remove the bananas from the pan and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil. Allow them to cool slightly, then serve while warm.
  6. Enjoy!
