Nilupak
Description
Nilupak is a traditional Filipino delicacy made from mashed starchy ingredients like cassava, banana, or sweet potato, mixed with sugar, butter, and condensed milk. The word nilupak means "pounded" or "mashed," referring to the way the ingredients are prepared. This sweet and chewy treat is popular as a merienda (afternoon snack) and is often topped with grated coconut or cheese for extra flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg cassava (peeled and boiled)
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup butter or margarine (softened)
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut (for topping)
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (optional)

Allergens

Dairy
Nilupak contains dairy which can cause issues for those with dairy allergies.

Steps

  1. Boil the cassava: Boil the cassava until soft, then drain and remove the tough fibers from the center.
  2. Mash the cassava: Using a mortar and pestle (or a fork/potato masher), pound or mash the cassava until smooth.
  3. Mix the ingredients: Add condensed milk, sugar, and butter to the mashed cassava. Mix well until smooth and fully combined.
  4. Shape the Nilupak: Transfer the mixture to a greased pan or shape it into small, round cakes.
  5. Add toppings: Sprinkle with grated coconut and cheese for extra flavor.
  6. Serve: Nilupak is best enjoyed fresh and slightly warm.
