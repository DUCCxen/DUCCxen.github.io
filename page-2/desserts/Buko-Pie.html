Buko Pie
Description
Buko Pie is a classic Filipino delicacy that originates from the province of Laguna. It is a rich, creamy coconut-filled pastry with young coconut meat (buko) and a slightly sweet, custard-like filling encased in a flaky, buttery crust. Unlike traditional fruit pies, buko pie does not use cream or condensed milk, relying instead on fresh coconut's natural sweetness and softness. Often enjoyed as a pasalubong (souvenir treat) from road trips to the southern Luzon region, buko pie is best served warm, highlighting its crisp crust and luscious filling.

Ingredients

  • For the crust:
  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (cold, cubed)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ cup cold water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • For the filling:
  • 2 cups young coconut meat (buko), shredded or sliced
  • 1 cup coconut water or evaporated milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch (dissolved in ¼ cup water)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Allergens

Dairy
Many recipes for Buko Pie include butter or milk as part of the filling or crust, which could cause reactions in those with dairy allergies or lactose intolerance.
Coconut
Coconut is classified as a tree nut by the FDA, though some people are allergic to it. Those with coconut allergies may experience reactions similar to other nut allergies.

Steps

  1. Mix flour, sugar, and salt in a bowl. Cut in the cold butter using a fork or pastry cutter until the mixture crumbles.
  2. Gradually add cold water, mixing until the dough forms. Then, divide the dough into two portions (one for the base, one for the top), wrap in plastic, and chill for 30 minutes.
  3. In a saucepan, combine coconut meat, coconut water (or evaporated milk), and sugar. Cook over low heat, stirring occasionally, until sugar dissolves.
  4. Add the cornstarch mixture and stir continuously until the filling thickens. Remove from heat, then stir in vanilla extract. Let it cool.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out one portion of the dough and fit it into a pie pan. Trim excess dough.
  6. Pour the cooled coconut filling over the crust.
  7. Roll out the second portion of dough and place it over the filling. Seal and crimp the edges. Poke holes or make slits on top to allow steam to escape.
  8. Bake for 40-45 minutes or until the crust turns golden brown.
  9. Let it cool slightly before slicing.
  10. Serve on a round plate. Enjoy.
