Sapin Sapin
Description
Sapin-Sapin is a popular Filipino kakanin (rice cake) known for its vibrant, multi-layered appearance and sweet, chewy texture. The name sapin-sapin means "layered" in Filipino, reflecting its distinct colorful layers, each infused with different flavors such as coconut, ube (purple yam), and jackfruit. Traditionally made with glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar, this delicacy is often topped with latik (coconut curds) or toasted coconut flakes for extra flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour (malagkit na harina)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 cups coconut milk (gata)
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup mashed ube (or ube flavoring)
  • 1/2 cup mashed ripe jackfruit (langka) or jackfruit extract
  • Food coloring (purple, yellow, and white)
  • Butter or banana leaves (for greasing)
  • 1/2 cup latik (coconut curds) or toasted desiccated coconut

Allergens

Dairy
Sapin Sapin uses Milk which could cause reactions in those with dairy allergies or lactose intolerance.
Coconut
Coconut is classified as a tree nut by the FDA, though some people are allergic to it. Those with coconut allergies may experience reactions similar to other nut allergies.

Steps

  1. Prepare the mixture: In a mixing bowl, combine glutinous rice flour, sugar, coconut milk, condensed milk, salt, and vanilla extract. Mix well until smooth.
  2. Divide the mixture: Divide the mixture into three equal portions.
  3. First layer (Purple/Ube): Mix ube or ube flavoring into one portion and add a few drops of purple food coloring.
  4. Second layer (Yellow/Jackfruit): Blend mashed jackfruit into another portion and add yellow food coloring.
  5. Third layer (White/Coconut): Leave the last portion plain for a natural coconut flavor.
  6. Prepare the pan: Grease a round or rectangular pan with butter or line it with banana leaves.
  7. Steam the layers: Pour the purple (ube) mixture first and steam for about 15 minutes. Once firm, pour the yellow (jackfruit) mixture on top and steam for another 15 minutes. Finally, pour the white (coconut) mixture and steam for another 15 minutes until fully set.
  8. Cool and garnish: Let the sapin-sapin cool completely before slicing. Sprinkle with latik or toasted coconut flakes for added texture and flavor.
  9. Serve: Slice into small squares or diamond shapes and serve on banana leaves for an authentic presentation.
