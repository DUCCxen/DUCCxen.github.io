Pichi Pichi
Description
Pichi-Pichi is a well-loved Filipino delicacy made from grated cassava, sugar, and coconut milk. This translucent, jelly-like kakanin has a soft and chewy texture and is usually coated with grated coconut or cheese for added flavor. Traditionally steamed, Pichi-Pichi is a simple yet delightful snack often served at parties, merienda (afternoon snack), or special gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups grated cassava (kamoteng kahoy)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • ½ teaspoon pandan extract (or 3 pandan leaves, boiled in water)
  • ½ teaspoon lye water (lihiya, optional for a firmer texture)
  • 1 cup grated coconut or cheese (for coating)

Allergens

Coconut
Coconut is classified as a tree nut by the FDA, although many people with nut allergies do not react to coconut. However, individuals with coconut allergies may experience symptoms similar to other nut allergies.

Steps

  1. Mix the ingredients: In a bowl, mix grated cassava, sugar, water, and pandan extract.
  2. Add lye water (optional): If using, add lye water and mix well (this helps give it a slightly firm and elastic texture).
  3. Pour into molds: Pour the mixture into small molds or a greased pan.
  4. Steam: Steam for about 40–50 minutes until the mixture turns translucent and firm.
  5. Cool and remove: Let the Pichi-Pichi cool slightly before removing from molds.
  6. Coat: Roll each piece in grated coconut or grated cheese, depending on your preference.
  7. Serve: Arrange on a plate and serve as a snack or dessert.
