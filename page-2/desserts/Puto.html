Puto
Description
Puto is a popular Filipino steamed rice cake that is soft, fluffy, and slightly sweet. Traditionally made from rice flour, sugar, and coconut milk, it has a light and airy texture, making it a favorite snack or side dish in Filipino cuisine. Puto is often enjoyed on its own or paired with savory dishes like Dinuguan (pork blood stew) to balance flavors. This delicacy comes in various flavors and colors, with some variations incorporating cheese, salted egg, or ube (purple yam) for added taste.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups flour (sifted)
  • 2 cups sugar (sifted)
  • 2 1/2 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/8 teaspoon Pandan essence
  • 1/2 cup butter (melted)
  • 1 piece egg (raw)
  • Small slices of cheese
  • 4 cups water

Allergens

Dairy
Puto contains evaporated milk. People with dairy allergies or lactose intolerance should be cautious.

Steps

  1. Combine all the dry ingredients, such as flour, sugar, and baking powder, in a mixing bowl.
  2. Add the egg, butter, pandan essence, and evaporated milk. Mix carefully.
  3. Pour the mixture into the molds.
  4. Pour water on the steamer and let it boil.
  5. Arrange the molds into the steamer and steam for about 20 minutes.
  6. Remove the cover of the steamer and put cheese on top of each puto. Put the cover back and let it steam for 2-5 minutes.
  7. Remove the molds from the steamer.
  8. Arrange on a plate. Enjoy.
