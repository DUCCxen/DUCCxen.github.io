Palitaw
Description
Palitaw is a traditional Filipino rice cake made from glutinous rice flour that is boiled until it floats—hence the name palitaw, which means "to float" in Filipino. Once cooked, these chewy, slightly sweet rice cakes are coated in grated coconut, sugar, and toasted sesame seeds, creating a delightful mix of textures and flavors. Palitaw is an easy-to-make snack or dessert, often enjoyed during merienda (afternoon snack) or festive gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour (malagkit na harina)
  • ¾ cup water
  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • ½ cup sugar (white or brown)
  • 2 tablespoons toasted sesame seeds

Allergens

Coconut
Coconut is classified as a tree nut by the FDA, although many people with nut allergies do not react to coconut. However, individuals with coconut allergies may experience symptoms similar to other nut allergies.

Steps

  1. Prepare the dough: In a bowl, mix glutinous rice flour with water until a smooth, pliable dough forms.
  2. Shape the dough: Scoop about a tablespoon of dough and roll it into a ball, then flatten it into a small oval or disc shape.
  3. Boil the dough: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Drop the flattened dough pieces into the boiling water.
  4. Cook until floating: Wait until they float to the surface (about 2–3 minutes), then remove them using a slotted spoon and drain excess water.
  5. Coat with coconut: Roll the cooked palitaw in grated coconut.
  6. Add toppings: Sprinkle with sugar and toasted sesame seeds for added flavor.
  7. Serve: Serve warm or at room temperature.
