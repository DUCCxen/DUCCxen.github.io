Pastillas de Leche
Description
Pastillas de Leche is a classic Filipino confection made from milk and sugar, shaped into bite-sized candies and traditionally wrapped in colorful paper. These soft, sweet, and creamy treats originated from the dairy-rich provinces of Bulacan and Laguna. Pastillas are often enjoyed as a dessert, snack, or pasalubong (souvenir treat), and some variations include flavors like ube, pandan, or even a hint of citrus.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh carabao’s milk (or substitute with full-cream cow’s milk)
  • 1 cup condensed milk
  • 1 cup powdered milk
  • ½ cup sugar (for coating)
  • 1 teaspoon butter (optional, for a richer taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Allergens

Dairy
Pastillas De Leche contains dairy which can cause issues for those with dairy allergies.

Steps

  1. Combine the milk: In a saucepan over low heat, combine fresh milk and condensed milk. Stir continuously to prevent burning.
  2. Simmer: Simmer until the mixture thickens into a soft dough-like consistency (about 20–30 minutes).
  3. Add powdered milk: Remove from heat and mix in the powdered milk and butter (if using). Stir until fully incorporated.
  4. Cool and shape: Let the mixture cool slightly until it’s easy to handle. Grease your hands with butter and shape the mixture into small cylinders or bite-sized logs.
  5. Coat with sugar: Roll each piece in sugar for a sweet outer layer.
  6. Wrap and serve: Wrap in colorful paper or cellophane for a traditional presentation. Pastillas can be eaten immediately or stored in an airtight container for up to a week.
