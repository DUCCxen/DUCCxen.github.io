Ube Kalamay
Description
Ube Kalamay is a deliciously sticky and chewy Filipino delicacy made from glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and ube (purple yam). This traditional kakanin (rice cake) is known for its vibrant purple color, sweet and nutty flavor, and smooth, chewy texture. It is often topped with latik (coconut curds) or toasted coconut flakes for extra richness. Ube Kalamay is commonly served during special occasions and fiestas but can also be enjoyed as an everyday treat.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour (malagkit na harina)
  • 1 ½ cups coconut milk (gata)
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 1 cup ube halaya (or ½ cup mashed ube with ½ cup sugar)
  • 1 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ube extract (for enhanced flavor and color)
  • For the Latik (Topping):
  • 1 cup coconut cream (kakang gata)

Allergens

Dairy
Many recipes for Buko Pie include butter or milk as part of the filling or crust, which could cause reactions in those with dairy allergies or lactose intolerance.
Coconut
Coconut is classified as a tree nut by the FDA, though some people are allergic to it. Those with coconut allergies may experience reactions similar to other nut allergies.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Latik: In a pan over low heat, simmer the coconut cream, stirring occasionally. Continue cooking until the oil separates and the coconut curds turn golden brown. Strain and set aside the latik for topping.
  2. Mix the ingredients: In a large pan or pot, combine glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, water, sugar, salt, and ube halaya. Mix well until smooth.
  3. Cook the mixture: Cook the mixture over low heat, stirring continuously to prevent lumps and sticking. Add the ube extract for a more vibrant color and stronger flavor.
  4. Thicken the mixture: Continue stirring until the mixture thickens and becomes very sticky (about 30–40 minutes).
  5. Transfer to a dish: Transfer the sticky ube mixture into a greased pan or banana leaf-lined dish. Flatten and smooth the top, then allow it to cool completely.
  6. Add toppings: Once cooled, top with latik or toasted coconut flakes.
  7. Serve: Slice into squares or wedges and serve.
