Dinuguan
Description
Dinuguan is a Filipino savory stew made from pork and pork offal cooked in a rich, dark gravy of pig blood, garlic, chili, and vinegar. This hearty dish is traditionally served with puto (rice cakes) and is a unique representation of Filipino cuisine's nose-to-tail cooking tradition.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork meat and offal, diced
  • 2 cups pork blood
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 2-3 Thai chilies
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil

Allergens

None
No common allergens, but contains pork and pork blood

Steps

  1. Boil water in a pot and add a pinch of salt. Put the macaroni in the pot and let it boil al dente.
  2. Once cooked, drain and set aside.
  3. For the meat sauce, cook the beef in a wok until the color turns brown.
  4. Add the onion and garlic. Once the onion softens, add the bell pepper. Saut√© for 1 minute.
  5. Pour in the water and the Filipino-style spaghetti sauce. Stir and cook for 12 to 15 minutes, then set aside.
  6. Start by melting butter in a pan for the white sauce. Add the all-purpose flour. Stir and cook for 1 minute.
  7. Pour milk slowly while stirring. Add half of the cheese and continue stirring until the sauce thickens.
  8. Mix the meat sauce and the macaroni. Then, pour the white sauce on top.
  9. Put the mixture in a baking pan and put the remaining cheese on top.
  10. Preheat oven to 350 F. Bake it for 15 minutes.
  11. Serve on a plate. Enjoy.
